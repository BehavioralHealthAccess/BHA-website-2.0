The gap

Directories fail the people who need them most

Ask any social worker, discharge planner, or care coordinator in NJ how they find a mental health bed or program: call a list that is half wrong, wait on hold, leave voicemails, repeat. Payer directories go stale; PDFs contradict APIs; nobody certifies which options are plausible for a given ZIP, insurance, and level of care.

That friction is not just annoying—it is a market failure. Teams need tools that respect what public data can prove, label uncertainty honestly, and cut the search space fast.

  • 📋 Stale or conflicting provider lists
  • ⏱ Hours lost to cold calls & dead ends
  • 🧩 No single “confidence” view across sources

What we build

A plausibility navigator—not a booking app

The data cannot schedule appointments. It can rank options you should try first: match filters, distance, and service fields; show explainable scores and short reasons; and (optionally) use AI to re-rank a short list from natural language—always on top of transparent rules.

ZIP & radius first

Anchor search on patient location, then filter by care type, setting, insurance, populations, and emergency services—same mental model coordinators already use.

Explainable match scores

Rule-based scoring from published facility fields—no black box for the default path.

Optional AI re-rank

Ask in plain English; the server sends a constrained prompt to Gemini to reorder a small pool—rules still visible underneath.

Map + detail pane

Scan geography quickly, then open phone, intake line, and structured service fields in one place.

Workflow

How it works in practice

  1. 1

    Enter location & constraints

    ZIP and radius set the geographic pool; filters narrow by level of care, insurance, populations, and crisis services.

  2. 2

    Review ranked results

    Facilities sort by match score and distance with short “why this row” explanations from field-level alignment.

  3. 3

    Optional: Ask AI in natural language

    Describe the case in prose; AI re-ranks a capped list from your current filter results—useful for messy, multi-criteria queries.

Roadmap

Public data we fuse over time

Today’s prototype centers on SAMHSA facility attributes. Next layers add corroboration and shortage context—without pretending any feed replaces verification with the facility.

NPPES

National Plan & Provider Enumeration System—provider identities, specialties, and practice addresses (large CMS file, monthly).

SAMHSA Live

Behavioral health treatment facility listings—core of the current navigator: services, settings, payor fields, and more.

HRSA

Mental health professional shortage areas—county-level pressure useful for triage storytelling and policy overlays.

Payer directory APIs

CMS transparency rules push machine-readable directories—future layer to cross-check “in network” claims responsibly.

CMS quality

Where available, inpatient psychiatric quality measures complement directory truth—not universal, but high signal.

Why now

Startup trajectory, not just a research exercise
